Summary

Initially, my focus was on comparing data relationships between different municipalities within Metro Boston, but no clear connections were identified. This led to a shift in approach towards examining the effects of zoning within individual municipalities, specifically comparing outcomes between different zoning types. This adjustment in focus aimed to uncover more nuanced insights into how zoning practices influence demographic and socio-economic patterns at a more localized level, revealing the importance of considering diverse analytical perspectives to fully understand the complexities of urban planning and zoning impacts.

Upon analyzing the data at the municipal level, it became evident that within the same municipality, different zoning regulations could significantly contribute to segregation in terms of demographics and income. This segregation indicates that zoning policies not only influence the physical landscape of neighborhoods but also have profound effects on the socio-economic fabric, leading to distinct demographic profiles and income levels within close geographical proximities. This insight underscores the critical role of zoning in shaping community composition, potentially exacerbating or mitigating socio-economic disparities.
